# TryHackMe vs HackTheBox: Honest Comparison for Beginners (2026)

If you're getting into cybersecurity and wondering whether to start with 
TryHackMe or HackTheBox, this guide is for you. I've used both platforms 
extensively for CTFs and skill building — here's my honest take.

## Quick Summary

| Feature | TryHackMe | HackTheBox |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Beginner friendly | ✅ Very guided | ❌ Sink or swim |
| Free tier | ✅ Generous | ✅ Limited |
| Paid plan | ~$14/mo | ~$14/mo |
| Cert prep (Security+/OSCP) | ✅ Yes | ⚠️ Partial |
| Community | ✅ Huge | ✅ Active |
| Best for | Total beginners | Intermediate+ |

## TryHackMe: Best for Beginners

TryHackMe holds your hand in the best way possible. Rooms walk you through 
concepts step by step, and the browser-based attack box means you don't even 
need to set up a local VM to get started.

### What I liked
- Pre-Security and Jr Penetration Tester paths are excellent
- In-browser terminal — no setup required
- Hints available when you're stuck
- Active Discord community

### What I didn't like
- Some rooms feel too guided (you're not really "hacking")
- Free tier has room limits

## HackTheBox: Best Once You Have Basics

HTB is where you go once TryHackMe has given you the foundations. Machines 
are realistic, challenging, and much closer to real pentest scenarios.

### What I liked
- Much more realistic challenges
- Pro Labs are excellent for OSCP prep
- Strong community writeups (after you solve it)

### What I didn't like
- Steep learning curve for total beginners
- Starting Point machines are good but limited on free tier

## My Recommendation

**Start with TryHackMe for your first 2-3 months.** Complete the 
Pre-Security path and Jr Penetration Tester path. Then move to HackTheBox 
once you're comfortable with basic enumeration and exploitation concepts.

This combo is the most efficient path to landing your first cybersecurity job 
or passing OSCP.
